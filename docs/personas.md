This document defines the core user personas for the Shelter App.  
These personas are based on real-world animal rescue workflows and were used to guide user flows, feature prioritization, and UI decisions.

---

## Persona 1: Foster Caregiver

### Name
Maria

### Role
Volunteer Foster

### Background
Maria fosters dogs for a local shelter while working a full-time job. She communicates with shelter staff, vets, and potential adopters across multiple channels including email, Facebook, and text messages.

### Goals
- Know exactly when vet appointments and meet-and-greets are scheduled
- Communicate clearly with the shelter without missing messages
- Easily share updates about the animal (health, behavior, photos)
- Avoid last-minute surprises or scheduling conflicts

### Pain Points
- Messages scattered across email, Facebook, texts, and calls
- Last-minute vet appointments or meet-and-greets
- No single source of truth for schedules or instructions
- Difficulty tracking what has already been communicated

### Needs from the App
- Centralized communication with the shelter
- Clear timeline of upcoming appointments and events
- Notifications that are timely and reliable
- Simple, mobile-first UI that fits into daily life

---

## Persona 2: Shelter Coordinator

### Name
Alex

### Role
Shelter Staff / Foster Coordinator

### Background
Alex manages multiple fosters, dozens of animals, and constant communication with volunteers, adopters, and veterinarians. Accuracy and speed are critical.

### Goals
- Coordinate fosters, adopters, and vets efficiently
- Ensure no appointments or follow-ups are missed
- Maintain clear records of communication and decisions
- Reduce manual work and repetitive follow-ups

### Pain Points
- Managing communication across many platforms
- Tracking who said what and when
- Scheduling conflicts between fosters and adopters
- Following up on adoption applications manually

### Needs from the App
- Central dashboard for animals, fosters, and adopters
- Scheduling tools for vet visits and meet-and-greets
- Visibility into communication history
- Confidence that everyone is seeing the same information

---

## Persona 3: Potential Adopter

### Name
Jordan

### Role
Adopter Applicant

### Background
Jordan is looking to adopt a dog and has filled out an application. They are excited but anxious about the process and timeline.

### Goals
- Understand the adoption process clearly
- Schedule meet-and-greets easily
- Receive updates without having to chase the shelter
- Feel confident and informed throughout the process

### Pain Points
- Long waits without updates
- Unclear next steps
- Confusion about scheduling or requirements
- Repeating information multiple times

### Needs from the App
- Clear adoption status and next steps
- Simple scheduling experience
- Transparent communication
- A calm, trustworthy interface

---

## How Personas Inform Design Decisions

- **Mobile-first UX** prioritized due to Foster usage patterns
- **Clear status indicators** reduce anxiety for adopters
- **Centralized communication** eliminates fragmented workflows
- **Design system consistency** supports trust and usability
- **Notifications & reminders** reduce missed appointments
